Mars Underground Habitat Module

Conceptual design of a sustainable, modular habitat for Mars colonization, integrating advanced storage, agriculture, laboratory, communication, and health systems.

Project Overview

This project presents a multi-module underground habitat designed for long-term human settlement on Mars. It addresses critical challenges such as radiation protection, resource recycling, and psychological well-being by offering integrated living, agricultural, and laboratory environments.

Design Highlights

  • Modular construction enabling easy expansion and reconfiguration.
  • Energy-efficient environmental control systems.
  • Advanced hydroponic agriculture module to sustain crew nutrition.
  • Laboratories equipped for scientific experiments and health monitoring.
  • Robust communication systems designed for interplanetary data exchange.

Technical Tools & Skills

The habitat was modeled using SolidWorks CAD, utilizing parametric design principles. Emphasis was placed on manufacturability, structural integrity, and modular adaptability.

Project Impact & Future Work

This concept contributes to the field of space habitat design by combining sustainability and functionality. Future work includes simulation of environmental conditions and integration with life support systems.
